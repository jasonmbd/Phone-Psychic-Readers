The Hanged Man Card - What It Shows

This card depicts a man hanging upside down with one foot tied to the living world tree. The roots of the tree reach deep, down to the underworld, and it is said to support the heavens. The man's expression is calm and serene, showing he hangs in that position by choice. The red pants he wears represent the physical body and human passion. He is not trapped - he is suspended, willingly, to see differently.

The Hanged Man Upright Meaning

Upright, The Hanged Man advises you to face the outcomes of your actions. You will learn from this experience and be grateful once it passes. It is the card of meaningful surrender - letting go of control and the rush to act so a new perspective can arrive. The pause is the point, not the problem.

The Hanged Man Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Hanged Man tells you to be prepared for a situation you'll go through. You will sacrifice something, but this one will not benefit the greater good. It cautions against martyrdom - giving something up that costs you without serving anyone, including you.

The Hanged Man as Advice

As advice, The Hanged Man counsels surrender over struggle: stop forcing the outcome, let go of the need to control it, and allow the shift in perspective that only stillness brings.
