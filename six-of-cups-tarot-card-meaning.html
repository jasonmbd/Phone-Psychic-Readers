Six of Cups - What It Shows

In a sunlit courtyard, an older child hands a cup filled with flowers to a younger one, five more cups of blooms nearby. The scene is gentle, safe, and sweet - a memory more than a moment. After the grief of the Five, the Six is comfort: the past offered not as a wound but as a flower. The water suit pauses here to remember tenderness.

Six of Cups Upright Meaning

Upright, the Six of Cups is nostalgia, innocence, and reunion. Warm memories surface, an old connection returns, kindness is exchanged with no agenda, or a simpler, gentler energy comes back into your life. Its core note is tenderness - the past as a gift, and the openness of a child's heart.

Six of Cups Reversed Meaning

Reversed, the past becomes a trap. Living in nostalgia, idealizing what was, refusing to grow up, or - more positively - finally releasing the past and stepping into the present. It can also flag naivety that needs maturing.

Six of Cups in Love & Relationships

In love, the Six of Cups very often signals someone from the past returning - an ex, a first love, a reconnection - or a sweet, innocent, low-pressure tenderness. Example: a caller asked if she'd hear from an old flame; the Six of Cups leaned yes on the reunion energy, with the honest caveat that the card romanticises the past, so she should weigh memory against present reality.

Six of Cups in Career & Money

For work it's a former contact, an old opportunity resurfacing, returning to something familiar, or working with a sense of generosity. Example: someone considering rejoining a former employer pulled this card; the message acknowledged the genuine pull while cautioning against nostalgia painting it rosier than it was.

Six of Cups as Advice

As advice: draw on the warmth of the past without living there. Reconnect, be generous and kind, honour good memories - then bring that softness into the present rather than escaping into the past.

Six of Cups Reversed in Love & Career

Reversed, this card deserves a closer look by area. In love, the cautionary read is clinging to an ex, idealizing a relationship that wasn't actually that good, or being stuck in "how it used to be"; the freeing read is finally letting the past go and being available to the present. In career, reversed it's outgrowing an old role or network, or refusing to move on from a former version of your work life. The reversed Six's question is whether the past is nourishing you or holding you. A live reader can tell you which it is here.

What People Ask About the Six of Cups

The questions readers hear most about this card:

  • "Does it mean an ex is coming back?"

    It's the classic "someone from the past" card - often yes, with context.

  • "Six of Cups as feelings?"

    Warm, nostalgic, "I remember us fondly" tenderness.

  • "Is it a soulmate or childhood-love card?"

    It can carry that long-history, familiar-soul feeling.

  • "Should I go back?"

    The card pulls toward it; reversed warns to check nostalgia vs. reality.

Six of Cups - Yes or No?

Upright: a warm yes, especially for reunions. Reversed: no, or "not if you're clinging to the past."

Six of Cups Timing in a Reading

Timing is the least precise thing tarot does, so treat this as a lean, not a date. As a water-suit six, readers usually place it within weeks, often tied to something or someone from the past resurfacing rather than a brand-new event. Reversed, the timeline stalls while you're stuck looking back. A live reader reads timing from the full spread and your question far more reliably than the card alone.

Six of Cups Card Combinations

Context changes everything - a few pairings readers see often:

  • Six of Cups + The Lovers

    A past love returning as a meaningful, values-aligned choice.

  • Six of Cups + Five of Cups

    Grief tangled with nostalgia - mourning the past.

  • Six of Cups + Eight of Cups

    Choosing to walk away from the past rather than return to it.
