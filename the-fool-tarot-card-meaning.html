The Fool Card - What It Shows

On The Fool tarot card, a young man stands on the edge of a cliff with a knapsack, ready to set out for a new adventure. He gazes up at the sky, unaware he is about to take a step and fall off the cliff. In his other hand he holds a white rose, showing his purity and innocence. At his feet is a small white dog representing loyalty and protection, encouraging him forward to face the challenges in front of him. The card reminds us to recall the path of personal development within ourselves - and the moment in which we recognize that path, so we can empower deeper self-realization.

The Fool Upright Meaning

Upright, The Fool is fresh starts and open-hearted possibility. It marks the beginning of a journey and an invitation to step forward with faith rather than fear. There is innocence here, and trust - the willingness to begin before you have every answer. When The Fool appears, a new chapter is opening and you're being asked to meet it with curiosity instead of overthinking it.

The Fool Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Fool means a person is still caught up in a phase of ignorance or immaturity. It can also show that an emotional reflex or a fixed psychological attitude is holding you back from reacting naturally or authentically to a situation. The lesson is being offered, but it isn't yet being met with openness.

The Fool as Advice

In the advice position, The Fool tells you to take matters easy and to think beyond the realm of logic. It instructs you to let go of demands and expectations and to pay attention to the events actually taking place in your life. Trust the step, even if you can't see the whole staircase.
