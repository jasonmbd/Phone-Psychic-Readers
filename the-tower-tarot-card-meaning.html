The Tower Card - What It Shows

The Tower depicts a high spire on top of a mountain. A lightning bolt strikes it and sets it ablaze, sending people leaping from the windows in desperation. The tower is a strong structure, but its foundation is weak - representing ambitions and goals set on false premises. The lightning is energy or insight that leads to a breakthrough or revelation. Twenty-two flames represent the twelve signs of the zodiac with the ten points of the Tree of Life, showing that even in disaster there is divine intervention.

The Tower Upright Meaning

Upright, The Tower represents change that arrives in the most radical and momentous way. It advises you to replace old foundations built on false premises with something more genuine that will serve you better in what's to come. It feels like collapse, but what falls is what couldn't have held. The shock is also the liberation.

The Tower Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Tower shows that the drama and problems are over. It advises you to leave behind all the issues and emotions that caused the bad situation to happen. The worst has passed; clinging to its wreckage is now the only thing keeping it alive.

The Tower as Advice

As advice, The Tower counsels you to let the false thing fall and rebuild true. Don't prop up a structure on a weak foundation; use the revelation to build something genuine that will actually hold.
