The Chariot Card — What It Shows

A man sits on a chariot drawn by two sphinxes — one black, one white — representing opposing forces. He carries the crescent moon on his shoulders, the spiritual influence that guides him. Although he is driving the chariot, he holds no reins, symbolizing that he steers with the power of his mind rather than force. On his chest is a square, denoting the element of earth, grounding both him and his actions.

The Chariot Upright Meaning

Upright, The Chariot tells you to overcome your challenges by maintaining control of your surroundings. Your willpower and determination are critical to getting past the obstacles in your path. This is the card of focused drive — opposing forces harnessed and pointed in one direction by sheer resolve. Victory here is earned through control, not luck.

The Chariot Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Chariot indicates a lack of control in your life — being at the mercy of opposing obstacles and challenges instead of steering through them. The forces are still there; the grip on them has slipped. It asks where you've handed your direction over to circumstance.

The Chariot as Advice

As advice, The Chariot counsels disciplined focus: take the reins with your mind, hold your direction steady against pulling forces, and let determination — not reaction — carry you forward.
