The Star Card - What It Shows

A woman kneels at the edge of a small pond holding two containers of water. From one she pours water onto dry land to ensure its fertility, shown by the green around her. One foot rests in the water - her spiritual abilities and inner strength - while the other rests on the ground - her practical sense. Behind her, a large star is surrounded by seven smaller ones, representing the chakras, with the large star representing her essence. Its astrological correspondent is Aquarius.

The Star Upright Meaning

Upright, The Star is hope, renewal, and quiet faith - the calm and healing that arrives after the upheaval of The Tower. As advice, it tells you to recommit to your higher values and to deepen your meditation practice; anything that interferes with that won't be serving your best interests right now. This is the card of being restored and gently guided.

The Star Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Star suggests you are temporarily unaware of your own brilliance and usefulness. It advises you to focus on your unique gifts and talents and to use them to meet whatever life puts in front of you. The light hasn't gone out; you've just lost sight of it.

The Star as Advice

As advice, The Star asks you to keep faith and tend your inner light: recommit to your values, make space for stillness, and trust that healing and renewal are genuinely underway.

As a Major Arcana card it points to a significant theme, not a passing detail.
