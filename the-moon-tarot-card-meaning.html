The Moon Card — What It Shows

The Moon is a symbol of intuition, dreams, and the unconscious. On either side stand a wolf and a dog, representing the animal nature of the mind — one wild, one civilized. A small pool represents the subconscious, and a crayfish crawling out of it represents the early stages of consciousness unfolding. Towers on both ends represent the forces of good and evil, alluding to the difficulty we have telling them apart by their similar appearance.

The Moon Upright Meaning

Upright, The Moon tells you to trust your instinct and intuition. This is the realm where logic can't fully see — dreams, hunches, and the things felt rather than known. The path is lit only dimly; the card asks you to feel your way with intuition rather than demand certainty the moonlight won't give.

The Moon Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Moon suggests you may be deluding yourself or exaggerating your version of a situation. It asks you to consider whether your account keeps to the bare facts, or has become an emotional, dramatic depiction. The fear is distorting the picture; clarity starts with separating the two.

The Moon as Advice

As advice, The Moon counsels honest discernment: trust genuine intuition, but check it against the facts so fear and imagination don't masquerade as insight.
