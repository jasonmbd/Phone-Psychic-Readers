The Devil Card - What It Shows

The devil is shown in his well-known satyr form, the Baphomet - half goat, half man, with bat wings and an overturned pentagram on his forehead. He stands on a pedestal with a naked man and woman chained to it. Both have horns, showing how time spent with the devil slowly turns them into his image. The man's tail is on fire, the woman's holds a bowl of grapes - symbolizing addiction to power and to the finer things. The chains around their necks are, crucially, loose: they're held against their will only because they don't notice the chains could easily be removed.

The Devil Upright Meaning

Upright, The Devil is bondage to attachment - addiction, obsession, a power dynamic, or a habit that has quietly taken your freedom. The image looks hopeless, but the loose chains are the whole point: the trap is real, and so is the exit. The card names the thing you've been pretending isn't holding you.

The Devil Reversed Meaning

Reversed, The Devil symbolizes a person becoming self-aware and breaking the chains that come with addiction or poor habits. These adjustments are difficult to make but pay off in the end. It's the moment the chains are noticed - and slipped.

The Devil as Advice

As advice, The Devil tells you to express your true emotions and acknowledge the feelings you have. Accepting the depth and power of your feelings helps you remain true to yourself - and seeing the attachment clearly is the first step out of it.
