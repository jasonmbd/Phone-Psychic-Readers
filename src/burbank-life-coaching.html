What Is Psychic Life Coaching?

Think of it as a regular life coach with an extra sense switched on. Psychic life coaching blends the practical side — goals, decisions, accountability, momentum — with intuitive insight a typical coach doesn't draw on. Some coaches use tarot or other tools to surface what you already know but haven't admitted; others work purely from intuition. Either way, the point isn't to be told your future. It's to put you back in touch with your own inner wisdom so the right move stops being a mystery.

How Is Psychic Life Coaching Different From Therapy?

This is the question people ask most, so let's be straight about it. Therapy is usually backward-looking and healing-focused, delivered by a licensed clinician — the right place for trauma, grief, and mental health. Psychic life coaching is forward-looking: it helps you get unstuck, set direction, and decide. A therapist might help you understand why you're still single; an intuitive coach helps you figure out what it'll take to change it. They're complementary, not competitors — and a good coach will tell you when therapy is the better call.

What Questions Should I Bring to a Life Coaching Call?

The questions that move you are open-ended — "what" and "how," not "should I" alone. "Should I take the job?" puts the decision on the coach; "What's actually behind my hesitation about this job?" puts it back in your hands where it belongs. Strong framings to bring:

  • "What am I not seeing here?"

    Surfaces the blind spot you're too close to catch.

  • "What's the fear underneath this?"

    Names the thing quietly steering the decision.

  • "What would I do if I trusted myself?"

    Cuts past the noise to what you already know.

  • "What's the smallest next step?"

    Turns a paralyzing decision into a doable one.

  • "Is this goal even mine?"

    Checks whether you're chasing something you actually want.

  • "What keeps me repeating this?"

    Finds the pattern under the recurring stuck point.

How Often Should You Work With a Life Coach?

It depends on the work. A single call is often enough to break a specific decision loose — many callers do exactly that and don't need another for months. If you're navigating a longer transition or trying to change a pattern that keeps resurfacing, a check-in every few weeks gives you accountability without dependency. Be wary of anyone who tells you that you need them indefinitely; good coaching makes you less reliant on it over time, not more. Come when you're at a real fork, not on a subscription someone talked you into.
