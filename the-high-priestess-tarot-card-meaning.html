The High Priestess Card - What It Shows

The High Priestess sits in front of a thin veil decorated with pomegranates, a symbol of abundance and fertility. She is positioned between two pillars marking the entrance to a sacred temple - one marked B for Boaz ("in his strength"), the other J for Jachin ("he will establish"). She wears a blue robe with a cross on the chest and an Isis moon crown, both signs of her divine knowledge and status as a divine ruler. In her lap she holds a scroll inscribed "TORA," and a crescent moon rests below her feet, symbolizing her relationship with the divine feminine.

The High Priestess Upright Meaning

Upright, The High Priestess asks you to honor your spiritual practice more regularly and to pay closer attention to the signs in your dreams - they may carry greater meaning than you've allowed. This is the card of intuition over analysis: the answer you're seeking is already known to you beneath the surface, if you'll get quiet enough to hear it.

The High Priestess Reversed Meaning

Reversed, she symbolizes ignorance or a lack of understanding. It points to a person ignoring a natural intuition that ought to be heeded - overriding the inner voice with noise, logic, or other people's opinions.

The High Priestess as Advice

As advice, The High Priestess counsels stillness and trust in your own knowing. Don't force the answer through reasoning alone; create space - reflection, rest, dream-attention - and let the understanding surface on its own.
